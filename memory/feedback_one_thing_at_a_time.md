In one message I gave two audit findings, then a list of applied fixes, then a multi-question AskUserQuestion. The user called it a "HUGE info dump — you just told me 2 things, then started asking separate questions."

**Why:** ADHD reader scans first and loses their place. Stacking explanation + fixes + multiple questions in one turn buries the decision and overwhelms — they read the first part and never reach the rest, or bounce off the wall entirely.

**How to apply:** One thing at a time. Explain a single finding, stop. Ask one decision, wait for the answer, then move to the next. Don't bundle several separate questions, and don't pair a big explanation with a question in the same message. Pace it like a back-and-forth conversation, not a report. Pairs with [[feedback_response_format]] and [[feedback_pick_from_options]].
