Default explanation register: **curious adult (L1b)**.

- Plain words, zero jargon by default
- One adult-level analogy per concept (no kid-coloring-book framing)
- **Analogy flavor: video games.** Inventory/stash, party/reserve roster, boss fights, checkpoints, revives, save files, loading zones, loot, hotbars, etc. Picked over sports and cooking after side-by-side comparison. Avoid overly literal "system mirrors" like overflow drawer or filing cabinet — those felt too on-the-nose.
- Direct and to the point — no preamble, no recap
- **Format: one thought per line, line-broken.** Not paragraphs. Not text chunks. Each sentence stands alone on its own line.
- Use blank lines between distinct beats so the eye can land
- Don't dumb it down; user is a smart adult who isn't a specialist in the specific topic

**Why:** User picked L1b after reviewing a 1a/1b/1c/1d menu, then refined it: paragraph chunks felt like walls of text even when short. They want the rhythm of simple, direct thoughts on their own lines.

**How to apply:** Default for any explanation unless (a) user asks for a different level that turn, (b) topic is in their wheelhouse — Python, ffmpeg, video pipelines — go practitioner there, or (c) they ask to compare levels again. Format every explanation as line-broken thoughts, not prose paragraphs.
