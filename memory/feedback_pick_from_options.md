When designing or wording anything non-trivial — a format, a rule, a phrase, a structure — show 3-4 distinct concrete variants and ask the user to pick. Do not ask "what tone do you want?" or "how should this be structured?" — the user can't articulate it cold but recognizes it instantly when shown.

**Why:** ADHD brain optimizes for recognition over recall. Open-ended "what do you want?" questions stall; concrete options unblock instantly. Confirmed across multiple turns — every time the user got stuck on phrasing, "show me examples" was the unstick.

**How to apply:** for any decision that's a matter of taste or phrasing (not a fact-finding question), default to producing 3-4 labeled variants A/B/C/D, visually separated, each one self-contained enough to evaluate without comparison-reading. Use the user's own prior phrasing as one of the options when possible — they often pick the version closest to what they already wrote.
